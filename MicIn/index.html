The Waveform

As a sound wave travels through the air particles are compressed and stretched apart. A sound at one specific frequency produces a simple soundwave with a constant wavelength.

The white line jumping up and down in the middle of the frame is the shape of all the sounds hitting the microphone on your computer over a very short timeframe. Sound travels through air like a wave as particles are compressed together and then stretched apart. By measuring how these particles change we can represent sound as a series of waves called a waveform.

The shape of a sound wave is defined by its wavelength, or the distance between two successive peaks or troughs, and its amplitude, the distance between the middle point of the wave and a peak or trough. The amplitude of a sound wave is proportionate to the volume of the sound— the greater the amplitude, the louder the sound. The wavelength of a sound wave traveling through air is proportionate to the pitch of the sound we hear, or the frequency at which the wave vibrates. A wave with a shorter wavelength will have a higher frequency and therefore a higher pitch, while a wave with a longer wavelength will have a lower pitch.

The waveform of a sound that vibrates at one frequency looks like a simple sine wave. This wave has a constant wavelength (Play the specific frequency tones to observe what the waveform of a constant frequency looks and sounds like). However, when many sounds are vibrating at different frequencies a waveform can look very bumpy and irregular. This is because this waveform is composed of all the individual sound waveforms added together.

A complex waveform is made up of simple waveforms vibrating at different frequencies and amplitudes.

Frequency Spectrum

The bars coming up from the bottom of the frame represent the frequencies of all the sounds that compose the current waveform. The values increase from left to right. That is, values at the left-hand side of the graph are low frequency sounds (low pitch) while values at the right-hand side of the graph are high frequency sounds (high pitch).

To observe the frequency of the sounds that make up our waveform we analyze the frequency spectrum. A frequency spectrum graph shows the volume of a range of frequencies— low to high frequencies from left to right. Each bar in the graph represents a unique frequency, or group of frequencies, and the height of that bar indicates the current volume of that frequency.

Sound frequency is measured as the number of times a sound wave cycles from peak to peak, or trough to trough, over a given time. Typically sound is measured in Hertz (Hz), the number of cycles per second. A sound at 200 Hz vibrates much slower than a sound at 3,000 Hz. The frequency of a sound wave gives the sound its pitch. Lower frequency sounds have a low pitch while high frequency sounds have a high pitch. Humans can hear sounds in the range of approximately 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz. You can play the specific frequency tones to hear and see different frequency sounds.
